Highlights of Pahiyas Festival

Major highlights include the house-decorating contests, the "Parikitan" parade of colorful costumes, the Kiping competition, and the vibrant street food scene featuring Lucban specialties like pancit habhab and longganisa.

History of Pahiyas

Pahiyas traces back to early farming traditions where farmers offered their harvest to San Isidro. It evolved into a colorful street festival celebrating faith, abundance, and creativity.

The Art of Kiping

Kiping is an edible, colorful rice wafer crafted by Lucbanins into elaborate decorations, symbolizing both artistry and thanksgiving spirit.

Modern Day Pahiyas

Today, Pahiyas continues to attract tourists and photographers worldwide, showcasing the Filipino spirit of gratitude, resilience, and creativity.
