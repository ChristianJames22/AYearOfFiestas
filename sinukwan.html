The Sinukwan Festival celebrates the culture, traditions, and mythological roots of the Kapampangan people. It honors "Aring Sinukwan," the mythical king believed to be the deity of ancient Pampanga, symbolizing the strength and unity of the Kapampangans.

History of Sinukwan

The festival was established to revitalize Pampanga's cultural pride after the devastation of Mount Pinatubo's eruption, reconnecting the people to their roots and mythology.

Aring Sinukwan Legend

Aring Sinukwan symbolizes the wisdom, strength, and leadership of the Kapampangan people, often depicted as a towering figure in grand floats and parades.

Modern Sinukwan Festival

Today, the Sinukwan Festival promotes cultural pride not only in Pampanga but throughout the Philippines, inspiring younger generations to cherish their heritage.
